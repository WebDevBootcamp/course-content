Garlic Cheese Grits

  • 6 cups cold water
  • 1 tsp. garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 cups hominy grits
  • 1 tbs. yellow onion, minced
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 8 oz. Velveeta, cubed
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  1. Preheat oven to 350°.
  2. Bring water to a boil. Add grits and return to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat and simmer about 5 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat, add all other ingredients, and put into oiled casserole pan.
  5. Bake covered for about 45 minutes, stirring midway.

Makes 10 big servings.

Texas Caviar

Cowpeas into caviar. Anything is possible in Texas. This simple dish proves the perfection of canned black-eyed peas. The onion and bell pepper provide a delicate crunch, the vinegar a slight tang.   • 2 quarts canned black-eyed peas, drained
  • 1 cup yellow onion, diced
  • 1 cup red onion, diced
  • 1/2 cup green bell pepper
  • 1 cup Vinegar and Oil diced Dressing (see Cookbook page 44)
  • 2 tsp. garlic, minced (1 tsp. is enough for some folks)
  • 1 tbs. Vegetable Seasoning (see Cookbook page 39)
  1. Mix all ingredients well.
  2. Marinate 8 to 12 hours, stirring a few times to coat all ingredients well.

Makes 2 1/2 quarts.

Babinka's Potato Salad

  • 4 Large potatoes (to be diced after boiling)
  • 4 hard-boiled eggs, diced
  • 3 heaping tbs. mayonnaise
  • 1 heaping tbs. mustard
  • 1/2 or more large pickle, diced
  • 1/2 cup or more cooked peas
  • dash Lawrey's (or other) Season Salt, to taste
  1. Peel the potatoes, boil them for forty minutes or slightly more. They should be firm enough to dice them (without mashing them).
  2. Allow the potatoes to cool before trying to dice them. When dicing, the chunks can be fairly coarse.
  3. Dice the eggs and pickles. These chunks should be smaller than the potato chunks, perhaps the size of large peas.
  4. Mix all ingredients in a large bowl.
  5. Adjust the mayo, mustard, and salt, to taste.
